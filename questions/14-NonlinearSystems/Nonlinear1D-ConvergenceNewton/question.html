Which of the following statement(s) is(are) true regarding the computational cost of finding an $x$ satisfying $f(x)=0$ for some function $f$?

Newton's method usually converges faster than bisection method. Newton's method will find an (approximate) solution of $f(x)=0$ irrespective of the starting guess $x_0$. A step in Newton's method can always be carried out for any smooth function and any value of the current guess $x_k$. Newton's method will always make progress towards a solution. It will always converge quadratically. It will always converge quadratically or linearly. It may converge quadratically, linearly, or not converge at all. It will either converge quadratically or not converge at all - no other outcome is possible.
