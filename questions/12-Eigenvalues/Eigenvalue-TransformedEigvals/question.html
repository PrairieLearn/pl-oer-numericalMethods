Suppose the matrix ${\bf A}$ has eigenvalues $\lambda_{A1} = {{params.e1}}$ and $\lambda_{A2} = {{params.e2}}$.

Let ${\bf I}$ be the identity matrix. The matrix ${\bf B}$ is defined by the following transformations on ${\bf A}$:

$${\bf B} = (({\bf A} - {{params.coe}}{\bf I})^{-1})^2$$

What are the eigenvalues of ${\bf B}$? ($|\lambda_{B1}| \geq |\lambda_{B2}|$)

To solve this question you need to understand how to determine the eigenvalues of a shifted matrix, eigenvalues of an inverse matrix, and eigenvalues of $\hat{A}^2$ (similar to Power Iteration). See eigenvalues section of the course notes.
