A $3 \times 3$ matrix $\mathbf A$ has eigenvalues $\lambda_1 = {{params.lm_1}},\ \lambda_2 = {{params.lm_2}},\ \text{and } \lambda_3 = {{params.lm_3}}$. An eigenvector corresponding to $\lambda_3$ is given by $\mathbf u = [{{params.u1}}, {{params.u2}}, {{params.u3}}]^T$. (Notice that $\|\mathbf u\|_{\infty} = 1$.) Suppose we perform inverse iteration, and normalize with respect to the infinity norm, $\|\cdot\|_{\infty}$. The initial guess $\mathbf{x}_0 = [{{params.x1}}, {{params.x2}}, {{params.x3}}]^T$ is used.

a. What is the approximate error $\|\mathbf{x}_{{params.iters}} - \mathbf{u}\|_{\infty}$ after performing {{params.iters}} iterations of normalized inverse iteration?

b. Suppose we start with the same initial guess but use shifted inverse iteration with shift $\sigma = {{params.shift}}$. What is the approximate error $\|\mathbf{x}_{{params.iters}} - \mathbf{u}\|_{\infty}$ after {{params.iters}} iterations?

To solve this question you need to understand convergence rates of shifted inverse iteration. This rate does not just depend on the smallest eigenvalue of $A-\sigma I$, but rather the ratio of the two smallest eigenvalues. See eigenvalues section of the course notes.
