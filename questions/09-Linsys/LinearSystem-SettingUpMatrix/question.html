Suppose you're the owner of a smoothie shop and that you have three fruits available to make smoothies: bananas, blueberries, and strawberries. You sell three types of smoothies, and each type requires a different amount of each fruit. See below the ingredient list for each smoothie:

Classic: {{params.a}} bananas, {{params.b}} cups of blueberries, and {{params.c}} cup of strawberries

Blue: {{params.d}} cups of blueberries and {{params.f}} cups of strawberries

Strawnana: {{params.e}} bananas and {{params.g}} cups of strawberries

At the end of the day you have used the following ingredients: {{params.bananas}} bananas, {{params.blueberries}} cups of blueberries and {{params.strawberries}} cups of strawberries.

To figure out how many smoothies of each type were sold during the day, you set up the linear system:

$ \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & 0 & a_{13} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} & 0\\ {{params.c}} & a_{32} & {{params.g}}\\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} {{params.bananas}} \\ {{params.blueberries}} \\ {{params.strawberries}} \end{bmatrix}. $

What is the entry ${{params.outputlabel}}$ in the matrix?

Using the system above, you determined that you sold {{params.Nclassic}} Classic smoothies. How many {{params.label2}} smoothies did you sell?
