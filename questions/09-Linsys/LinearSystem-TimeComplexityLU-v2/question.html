The Parks and Rec department has an opening for a Solver of Linear Equations. Since Leslie is great at solving systems of linear equations she zealously offers to give all candidates a quick demonstration.

She finds the LU factorization of a ${{params.n0}} \times {{params.n0}}$ matrix ${\bf M}$ in {{params.t0}} seconds. She then takes another {{params.t1}} seconds to apply both triangular substitutions (backward and forward) to solve the system of equations ${\bf M x} = {\bf y}$ for a given vector ${\bf y}$.

The candidates are supposed to estimate (without actually running any code) the time it takes to factorize and efficiently solve a similar system of linear equations ${\bf A x} = {\bf b}$, where ${\bf A}$ is a square matrix with dimension ${{params.n1}}$, given ${{params.b}}$ different right-hand side ${\bf b}$ vectors. Provide your time estimate $t$ in the answer box below.
