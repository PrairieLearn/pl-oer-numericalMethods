Suppose you are given the following function $f$ and asked to to find a minimum of it. $$f(x, y) = 3 + \frac{x^2}{8} + \frac{y^2}{8} - \sin(x)\cos\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}y\right)$$ To do so, first we must construct the gradient of $f$. What is the gradient, evaluated at the starting point $\mathbf{x_0} = [\frac{\pi}{3}, \frac{\pi}{2 \sqrt{2}} ]$?

Now let us construct the Hessian $H_f$ of the function. What is the Hessian, evaluated at the starting point, $\mathbf{x_0}$?

Now, calculate the first iteration, $\mathbf{x_1}$, using Newton's Method.

Using the same initial condition, what is the first iteration of Steepest Descent? (use $\alpha = {{params.alpha}}$)
