Assume $\mathbf{v}$ is a vector in $\mathbb{R}^{{params.dimensions}}$. You wish to compute a vector $\mathbf{w}$ such that the $i$th entry of $\mathbf w$, denoted $w_i$, is the average of {{params.window}}. For example, {{params.example_entry}} is the average of {{params.example_window}}.

For elements at the endpoints of $\mathbf w$, you will account for all available elements in your average. For example, {{params.example_endpoint}} (assuming zero-based indexing) is the average of {{params.example_endpoint_window}}.

Construct a matrix $\mathbf{A}$ such that $\mathbf{A} \mathbf v = \mathbf w$. Fill in the entries of $\mathbf{A}$ below.

If $\mathbf{A}$ is stored in sparse COO format, with $64$-bit floats used for the data and $32$-bit integers for the indices, how many bytes does it takes to store $\mathbf{A}$?
