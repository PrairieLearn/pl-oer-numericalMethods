What is the minimum-norm solution to the least-squares problem:

$\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} & \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ 0 & \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} & \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} {{params.sv0}} & 0 & 0\\ 0 & {{params.sv1}} & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0\\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} {{params.Vh00}} & {{params.Vh01}} & 0 \\ {{params.Vh10}} & {{params.Vh11}} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \end{bmatrix} {\bf x} \cong $

If $\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{U}\mathbf{\Sigma}\mathbf{V}^T$ is the SVD, then the solution of minimal 2-norm is given by $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{V}\mathbf{\Sigma}^{+}\mathbf{U}^T\mathbf{b}$

If $\mathbf{\Sigma} = \text{diag}(\sigma_1,\sigma_2,\sigma_3,0)$, then $\mathbf{\Sigma}^{+} = \left(\frac{1}{\sigma_1},\frac{1}{\sigma_2},\frac{1}{\sigma_3},0\right)$

Take advantage of the zeros in the problem to save yourself some work.
