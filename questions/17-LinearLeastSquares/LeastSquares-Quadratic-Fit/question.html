Fitting with a Quadratic

When working on the previous problem, you probably realized that a linear line of best fit is a rather poor approximation for the data. You are given a table with the exact same data as the previous question. This time, you will use linear least squares to find the quadratic function of best fit through the data. (Note that there is no data from 2007.)

The data in the table is provided as two numpy arrays year and percent for your convenience.

Year 2005 2006 2008 2009 2010 2011 2012 2013 2014 2015
Percent 12 41 63 72 78 80 83 88 84 90

Hint: Numpy has a np.linalg.lstsq function for solving for the coefficients of the least squares problem.

Store the coefficients as c0, c1, and c2 such that your quadratic could be written as $y = c_0 + c_1x + c_2x^2$. Then, plot the data points and your quadratic of best fit on the same figure. The data should be plotted using plt.scatter while the quadratic of best fit should be plotted using plt.plot. Use the same $x$-values when plotting the data and line of best fit. Be sure to include an appropriate title and labels on your plot.
